What is a mini-skill? A single, self-contained atomic step — one gate evaluation, one shift, one bijection check. Example (skill bit_apply_rules): given a per-bit rule and a query byte, apply each rule position-by-position and read off the output. One verifiable micro-step that a full trace chains many of. The weight on each card is how often the builder samples that skill.
Provenance & honesty. These mini-skills were designed from holistic inspection of the model's mistakes — read what it got wrong, build a targeted drill for that gap. The framework registers 239 skills; this gallery covers 233 of them. The full random dump is miniskills_sample.jsonl (5,891 weighted-random rows, examples from 221 skills). These are raw generator output, answer/format-consistency filtered (we drop rows whose reasoning doesn't reach their boxed answer, remove exact-duplicate pairs, and exclude three known-ambiguous skills) — not a polished, fully-audited dataset.
